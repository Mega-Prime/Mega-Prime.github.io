What. When. How.

What often occurs in relationships are disagreements. Arguments. Some trivial, some more serious. But inevitably they will happen, and you must have the proper tools to deal with them.

When dicord happens, could be over words said, Actions that threaten the health of the relationship, or most common, a misunderstanding and or a miscommunication that takes a small flame of "upset" and fans it to full blown anger and all out war against one another.

I'm sure we've all experienced something, felt we had the right tools, and attempted to employ them to solve a problem. Maybe they helped. Maybe they didn't. But I surely can say in my experience, there have been times when I totally have been confused about what the disagreement was about, how we even got to that juncture, attempted to solve the problem logically, and defuse the situation only to make it worse. Possibly because of my own ignorance of the issue, or maybe something I didn't see in myself or other person that could have lead to an easier, quicker, and more amicable solution.

"How do we solve these problems Mega?" You ask. The answer to that question is "I don't know!" I can't solve every problem, and in a relationship, I surely cannot solve them all on my own. What I think is important to start with is good communication on all sides. Honesty and openness without judgment or finger pointing. But most importantly, and I think many, many people forget this, is to understand when you are in a relationship (Friendship, romantic, professional), and you have a disagreement, remember that it is you and the other person against the problem, not you against each other.

Give it some thought.

"I can't". "I'm not confident with"…Two phrases that I believe hold many people from achieving certain goals, and losing out on the opportunity to take hold of the wheel in driving along the path to their success.

Own your success! There are many roadblocks and people along the way to your greatness that will do many things, and put you in many situations for potential failure. Don't be one of those roadblocks. Building self awareness, confidence and trust will help propel you past these obstacles and reward you in more ways than one.

Owning your success doesnt mean you know everything. Doesn't mean you refuse to ask for help. Doesn't mean you have to do everything alone. It is quite frankly, the opposite. Owning your success means you know when to get a second opinion, pick up a book to learn something new, or enlist the wisdom of others to impart on you new knowledge that can help in sculpting a better version of you.

Owning your success means holding yourself and others that are a part of your journey accountable.

Do that, and surely you can be confident you will SUCCEED.

Constructive vs. Destructive criticism

Have you ever given someone feedback on something they were a part of? Or maybe you were the recipient of the feedback or the tail end of an expressed opinion. How did it feel?

There are times where we will need to express our opinions or provide feedback to others on their performance in many situations in life. But the only way to be an effective communicator is to understand the difference between constructive and destructive criticism. Often enough these two forms of critique are confused and mis-used.

Constructive criticism provides pathways to learning and understanding of how one can turn weaknesses into strengths. Allows for growth, and let's a person walk away feeling confident they can solve a problem with real solutions with the right support and tuning of their efforts.

Destructive criticism is the exact opposite. It's draining, often de-moralizing and de-motivating. Always remember, blowing out someone else's candles won't make yours brighter! Help build bridges to learning, not walls to block progress.

The Un-appreciated Value

Have you ever felt like the Unappreciated value in a relationship, Romantic, platonic or otherwise? You say, "well what do you mean by that Mega?" What I mean is have you ever been the "Appreciative value" for someone? You add value to their lives. You show up like a friend should. You love them how they want and need to be loved. You give. You compromise. You give some more. Yet you feel all of that goes unnoticed, and you feel all of your efforts go un-reciprocated. Not that you do those things for the recognition or anything in return, but some positive affirmations would be nice.

I was asked how I handle those feelings. Those times where I feel like I am giving so much, and still feeling like the value I add and efforts I put in to help lift others up goes unappreciated. I feel used. At work. At home. In the world. No one wants to feel used.

My answer to that is simple. Control what you can. YOU. Sometimes you have to love, but give a little less. Know the level of rapport you have with someone. Know the level of selfish you are willing to accept from others. Know your limits on what you are willing to let go unnoticed, even if it's just a few words of positive affirmation from others. Know how to say 'No' if those you care for are not willing to step up and be an 'Appreciative Asset' in your life. Relationships on any level are a give and take. But on all levels, the giving should be equal in effort!!

Accepting Change

As humans we are creatures of habit. We have tendencies to repeat things over and over knowing these actions yes can result in positves/successes, but more often than not, these habits lead to failures, pain, loss (opportunity, time, relationships), and even freedom.

When we are unsure, or feel as if we are losing control, we run to a place of comfort. Think of it like a child with a teddy bear or a blanket. Often psychologically, we trick ourselves into believing what we are doing is the safest thing for us. We are naturally opposed to change.

Learning to accept change, and seeing the outlook of others as opportunities to learn, can not only inspire growth within self, but can and will expand knowledge, build positive rapport, and show that you are flexible and open minded.

Being able to see past your own agendas, and work toward a collective agenda for success, very much requires the ability to ACCEPT CHANGE.
